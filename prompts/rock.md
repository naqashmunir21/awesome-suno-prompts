## Production Tips

### 🎛️ **Guitar Tones by Subgenre**

| Subgenre | Distortion Level | Amp Type | Notable Effect |
|----------|------------------|----------|----------------|
| **Stadium Rock** | High (8/10) | Marshall stack | Arena reverb |
| **Grunge** | Very High (9/10) | Vintage tube | Chorus pedal |
| **Classic Rock** | Medium (6/10) | Fender/Marshall | Analog warmth |
| **Metal** | Extreme (10/10) | Mesa Boogie | Tight gate |
| **Punk** | High (7/10) | DIY amps | Raw distortion |
| **Indie** | Low-Med (3/10) | Clean Fender | Jangle EQ |

### 🎤 **Vocal Characteristics**

- **Stadium Rock:** Powerful, soaring, wide vibrato, multi-tracked harmonies
- **Grunge:** Raw, angsty, whisper-to-scream dynamics, emotional delivery
- **Classic Rock:** Bluesy raspy tone, sustained notes, soulful phrasing
- **Metal:** Operatic highs OR guttural growls, technical precision
- **Punk:** Shouted, raw, attitude-focused, group gang vocals
- **Indie:** Intimate, conversational, unique character, vulnerable

### 🥁 **Drum Production**

- **Arena Rock:** Massive reverb, gated snare (80s), powerful toms
- **Grunge:** Loose timing, natural room sound, heavy on cymbals
- **Classic Rock:** Vintage Ludwig sound, jazz-influenced fills
- **Metal:** Triggered samples, double-kick precision, blast beats
- **Punk:** Simple, fast, raw, minimal fills
- **Indie:** Natural kit, minimal processing, vintage warmth

### 📊 **Typical BPM Ranges**

- **Stadium Rock:** 130-150 BPM (anthemic)
- **Grunge:** 90-130 BPM (varies widely)
- **Classic Rock:** 110-140 BPM (groove-based)
- **Metal:** 140-220 BPM (genre-dependent)
- **Punk:** 160-200 BPM (fast and furious)
- **Indie Rock:** 120-145 BPM (moderate)
